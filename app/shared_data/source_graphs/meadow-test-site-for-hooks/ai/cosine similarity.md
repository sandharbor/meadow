Cosine similarity is a measure used to determine the similarity between two vectors in a multi-dimensional space. It calculates the cosine of the angle between the vectors, which ranges from -1 to 1. 

In the context of natural language processing, cosine similarity is often used to compare the similarity between two documents or texts. Each document is represented as a vector, where each dimension corresponds to a specific term or word. By calculating the cosine similarity between these vectors, we can determine how similar the documents are in terms of their content or meaning. A value of 1 indicates that the documents are identical, 0 means they are completely dissimilar, and negative values imply an inverse relationship.
