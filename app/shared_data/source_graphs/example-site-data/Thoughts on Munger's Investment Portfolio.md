These are my private notes on [[Charlie Munger]]'s known portfolio positions and investment rationale.

## Notable Holdings

- **Costco (COST)**: Munger served on the board for decades. His thesis centered on the moat created by membership loyalty and extreme operational efficiency. He considered it a textbook [[Circle of Competence]] play.
- **BYD Company**: An early and contrarian bet on Chinese electric vehicles. Munger saw the founder, Wang Chuanfu, as a once-in-a-generation operator. This position was a departure from typical Berkshire-style investments.
- **Daily Journal Corp (DJCO)**: Munger chaired this company and concentrated its portfolio into a handful of high-conviction positions, including Bank of America and Wells Fargo.

## Personal Takeaways

Munger's portfolio is surprisingly concentrated — far more so than most professional investors would be comfortable with. He genuinely practices what he preaches about conviction and patience. Worth revisiting when I'm tempted to over-diversify.
