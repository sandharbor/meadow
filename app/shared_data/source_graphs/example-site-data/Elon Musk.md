Elon Musk (born 1971) is the founder of SpaceX and Tesla, known for applying [[First Principles Thinking]] to industries that most people assumed were fixed.

## First Principles in Action

When Musk wanted to build rockets, he was quoted prices of $65 million per launch. Instead of accepting this, he asked: what are rockets actually made of? Aluminum, titanium, copper, carbon fiber. The raw materials cost roughly 2% of the sticker price. So he started SpaceX to build rockets from scratch.

The same approach drove Tesla. Rather than assuming batteries would always be expensive, Musk broke the cost down to raw materials and found a path to dramatic cost reduction.

## Connections

Musk's approach illustrates several mental models working together:
- [[First Principles Thinking]] to question assumptions
- [[Second Order Thinking]] to anticipate how cheaper rockets would reshape the space industry
- [[Margin of Safety]] in engineering — SpaceX over-tests components to account for unknowns
- [[Feedback Loops]] — each launch provides data that improves the next one
