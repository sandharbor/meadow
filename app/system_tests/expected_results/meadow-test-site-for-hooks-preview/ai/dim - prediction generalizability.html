    In the context of machine learning, "training generalization" refers to the model's ability to apply information or patterns it has learned from the training dataset to new, unseen data. A model that generalizes well is able to make accurate predictions or decisions when presented with new data that it wasn't trained on. This is a crucial aspect of machine learning, as the ultimate goal is to create models that can perform accurately and reliably on real-world data, not just the specific examples they were trained on.
