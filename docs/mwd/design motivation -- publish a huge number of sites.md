^ [[design motivation]] -- publish a huge number of sites

---

We don't want to be precious with publishing.  In [[design motivation -- speed]] we talk about [[publish any site in less than a minute]].  That also means that we will have a _lot_ of [[site]]s piling up, and we'll need to manage them.

Sophisticated [[multi site management]] plays a big role here, as does directly dealing with the challenge of [[managing changes in the source graph structure over time]].  But also, because of  [[design motivation -- self-contained partitions]], and [[design motivation -- static files]],  [[once you publish a site, you can leave it alone]], which means you don't necessarily need to consider it.  So, after publishing a site you can move it to [[site state -- archived]] to remove it from your primary list.

Related:
* [[design motivation -- speed]] (if you're going to publish hundreds of sites, each must be quick to publish)
* [[design motivation -- reviewability]] (Really good [[meadow change management]] tooling leads to [[dim - trust -- high|high trust]], which lets someone move fast, allowing them to make hundreds of sites)
